ABOUT THE PROJECT

This case study focuses on understanding the difficulties and challenges faced by users of Gogest, a SaaS business management software, and finding solutions to improve their experience.

  • Industry

    IT/ SaaS

  • Date

    February-March 2024

  • My role

    Ux researcher and UX Designer

Main goal

Through a detailed analysis, we seek to map the main points of frustration and identify opportunities for improvement, ensuring that Gogest better meets the needs of its users and stands out in the market. The goal is to implement improvements that make Gogest more accessible, efficient and pleasant to use, increasing customer satisfaction and the effectiveness of the software.

Discovery/process

After speaking with the company's manager and CEO to understand the problem and challenges, it was decided to interview Gogest users and try to understand what was really happening.
Background

Gogest is a business management solution designed to optimize data integration and sharing via the Cloud. This versatile platform, accessible via web and mobile devices, offers a complete set of management features, including CRM and support for sales and technical teams. Compatible with renowned systems such as PHC, Primavera, Sage and XD, Gogest guarantees efficient and essential integration to maximize the effectiveness in managing any type of business.

Problem

There was a drop in software usage due to software changes and the introduction of the mobile version. In addition, people in the customer support department noted an increase in complaints and bugs when using the software.

How I'm going to solve this?

Challenge

The software was created for small and medium-sized companies and depending on the company's area and employees' background, there is a limitation in its use by employees, as they are older people and have difficulty adapting to technology.

Goals and objectives

Increase software usage and increase the number of customers

Problem definition

A qualitative method was chosen to understand the needs and objectives to validate the initial assumptions.

What we want to find out
  • - Why has the number of complaints to customer service increased?
  • - Why don't all employees of the companies that sold the software use Gogest?
  • - What is the adhesion to the software? Or what they think of the software?
  • - Is the software intuitive?
Hypotheses
  • - Changing the software navigation will make it easier to use
  • - UX writing :If change the technical terms in the software and use more simple words, it will be easier to find what the user is looking for
  • - Redesign the UI: Better colors to read better and more accessible

LET’S DO SOME RESEARCH

Desk research

A competitive analysis of Gogest's competitors was carried out. Several software programs were tested to make a side-by-side comparison and try to understand what differentiates Gogest from the competition's software.

User research
  • - Stakeholders interviews: 2
  • - Support customer and techicians interviews: 4
  • - User interviews: 7

Seven people from different companies were invited to understand the user experience and how to improve and increase daily use of the software. Of the seven interviews, four were via video call and three were in person. Each interview lasted 1 hour, except for two interviews that lasted 1 hour and 30 minutes. A semi-structured interview was adopted, as it allows for a more assertive approach to the interview and gives the interviewee some freedom to talk about things that are not in the script, which can provide new insights for the research.

Findings/insights
  • - Users find it difficult to navigate the software because they have difficulty performing tasks, cannot find them or have too many steps to complete the task.
  • - Users praised the fact that everything is concentrated in one place and they don't need to go to multiple platforms to carry out their daily management tasks
  • - Slowness in the software and reported bugs that are not fixed
  • - Lack of support from the company in using the software, as a good portion of those interviewed created internal manuals for using the software
Scopping

After the interviews, the consistent complaint across all interviewees was that the software was complicated to use, so it was decided to redesign the software to be more intuitive and accessible to users.

Solution design

Design system

IBM’s Carbon design system was used to make it quicker and easier to create high-fidelity wireframes, and IMB Carbon allows us to create excellent, accessible user experiences.

Final design

How did I solved the user needs and problems?

" I can'find anything, it's hard to navigate"

  • - First approach: how to improve readability? By changing the information architecture in the header and menu, making navigation more intuitive.
  • - I simplified the menus. The menus had several levels, so I reduced them and made the menu smaller. For example, in analyses there were several options for analysis types, so I removed them to just one and when you open the analyses screen, instead of always clicking on the menu, you can navigate the same page through tabs.
  • - In addition, the entire UI was redone. The old version used shades of red, which had no contrast or readability. In the new version, colors were used that were easier to read and accessibility practices were adopted.
  • - Some search fields were strategically added to make it easier for the user to search, something that was not present anywhere else in the software. They were added to the header, the menu sidebar and the pages that the user is browsing.
Meet Talkie Gest
  • - A chatbot was created to make it easier to use the software and to help resolve any difficulties.
  • - In addition to helping, Talkie Gest will also be an assistant that will present possible new features in the software, as this was one of the complaints from users who were not aware of the new features.
"I had to create gogest user manuals"

Most users complained that the software was not intuitive and they did not find the manual provided by the company or were not even aware of its existence, So companies created internal manuals. The solution to this problem was to first redesign the Help Center menu and add a "Tutorials" section. In this section, the online manual was added and a unique section for video tutorials/screen recording, was created.

Customers frequently call customer support to report a problem or to find out the status of the process.

In the old version, the customer would click on the Miscellaneous menu, Tickets section and exit the program, ending up on another page and not being able to find any information about the reported problem. In addition, they found it complicated to register a complaint, so they preferred to call to customer support. So a new section was created in my Help Center. On this screen, as you can see, you can report directly to Gogest and track the complaint. You can see whether the complaint has been registered or not, the status of the process and the status of the complaint.

NPS

NPS was created to determine the level of customer satisfaction with the software in general and also NPS for the reported bug/problem. This way, the company can better evaluate the level of customer satisfaction and collect feedback from users.

Next Steps

I would like to conduct usability testing to see if the new redesign meets expectations and corrects the software's flaws. This type of software tends to be somewhat complex due to the number of things that can be done and added depending on the client's needs. It would be interesting to conduct usability tests for each topic in the Menu, such as Customer management, CRM, technical assistance, Analysis, etc., to try to understand what difficulties users have and how to help them have a simpler and easier experience.
